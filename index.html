About me

As a BSc (Hons) Computer Science for Games student at the University of Brighton, I am deeply enthusiastic about game development. My commitment and work ethic are fueled by my fascination with how games offer both creative expression and a means of exploring diverse perspectives from around the world.

My professional background includes a decade in the automotive finance sector, where I've honed my skills in software and system change delivery. As a PRINCE2 certified project manager, I've contributed significantly at Santander Consumer Finance (UK) and PSA Group, overseeing teams and managing operations.

I am now eager to apply my extensive project management expertise and passion for gaming to a career in game design and development, aiming to innovate and excel in this dynamic field.

Project: UE5 Puzzle-Solving Shooter Action Game
Assignment Report

UE5 | Blueprints | Game Design

The brief for this assignment was to design and develop a prototype game using Unreal Engine 5. I decided to develop a puzzle-solving shooter action game. Having an open and unspecific brief meant that I could learn skills in visual blueprint scripting and Unreal Engine system as a whole. I decided to develop animation blend spaces and animation state machines for the character movement, develop a health system, pickup item system, gun reload system, and an enemy AI behaviour tree.
